## 🚀 Getting Started (Takes 2 Minutes)

**Step 1 — Open the app**
Double-click `family-tree.html`. It opens in Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari — all work fine.

**Step 2 — Load the existing data**
Click **📂 Load JSON** → select `family-tree.json`.
You'll see the family tree I've already built. Don't worry if it's incomplete — that's why I'm sharing this with you!

**Step 3 — Add your family members**
Use the form on the left side to add people. See the section below for details.

**Step 4 — Save and send back**
Click **💾 Save JSON** → a file called `family-tree.json` will download.
Send that file back to me by WhatsApp, email, or Google Drive.

## ✏️ Adding or Editing a Member

The form on the left side is where all the action happens.

### To add a NEW person:
Fill in the form fields and click **💾 Save**.

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Name** | Full name (e.g. *Ramesh Patel*) |
| **Birth Year** | Optional — just the year (e.g. *1952*) |
| **Gender** | Male / Female / Other |
| **Paternal Parent** | Their father (if that person is already in the tree, pick from dropdown) |
| **Maternal Parent** | Their mother (if she's in the tree, pick from dropdown) |
| **Spouse** | Their husband/wife (if already in the tree) |
| **Photo** | Optional — upload a photo from your device |

### To edit an EXISTING person:
**Click their name/box in the tree.** The form will fill with their details. Make changes and click **💾 Save**.

### To delete someone:
Click their box → the red **🗑 Delete** button appears at the bottom of the form.

## 👨 Paternal vs 👩 Maternal View

The toggle at the top switches between two views:

- **👨 Paternal** — shows the family tree through the father's side (grandfather → father → children)
- **👩 Maternal** — shows the family tree through the mother's side (grandmother → mother → children)

Use the **"View from"** dropdown to pick *whose* perspective you're looking at.
The default shows the youngest generation — you can change it to anyone.

> **Example:** Select "Jesal" + Maternal view → shows Jesal's mother's entire lineage going upward. Select Paternal → shows his father's lineage instead.

## 📋 Tips for Filling In Your Side

Here's the order that works best when adding a branch of the family:

1. Add the **oldest generation first** (great-grandparents)
2. Then their children (grandparents)
3. Then spouses of those children
4. Then the next generation down
5. For each married couple — add the spouse separately, then link them using the **Spouse** dropdown

> **For married-in relatives** (e.g. your bhabhi, your maama's wife): add them as a new person, leave Paternal/Maternal Parent blank, and just set the Spouse field.

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: I accidentally deleted someone. Can I undo?**
If you haven't saved the JSON yet, just close the tab and reopen `family-tree.html` + reload the JSON. If you already saved — sorry, no undo. Be careful with delete!

**Q: The name is showing short in the box. Is that normal?**
Yes — the box only shows the first name to save space. The full name is stored and shown when you click to edit.

**Q: Someone appears in the wrong position / the tree looks messy.**
You can drag any box to a better position. Or click **↺ Reset Layout** to let the app re-calculate the best positions.

**Q: I entered someone but they don't show up.**
Check the **"View from"** dropdown and the Paternal/Maternal toggle. The person might be on the other side or outside the current focal person's chain. Try switching to the other toggle.

**Q: Can two people share the same name?**
Yes — the app uses internal IDs so duplicate names are fine (e.g. two people named "Ramesh").
